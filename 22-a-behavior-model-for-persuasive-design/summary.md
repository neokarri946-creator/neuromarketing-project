# A Behavior Model for Persuasive Design (2009) — Fogg

Why do people fail to do things they genuinely want to do — exercise more, save money, sign up for a service they already like? BJ Fogg, a researcher at Stanford's Persuasive Technology Lab, argued that the problem was not a lack of desire but a misunderstanding of how behaviour actually works. Before his 2009 model, designers tended to focus on motivation alone: make people want it enough and they will act. Fogg showed this was incomplete and often wrong. He proposed that understanding the mechanics of behaviour was essential for anyone trying to design products, campaigns, or systems that reliably change what people do.

Fogg's model states that a behaviour occurs only when three elements converge at the same moment: Motivation, Ability, and a Trigger (B = MAT). Motivation is the person's desire to act — driven by pleasure, hope, or social acceptance. Ability is how easy the action is — determined by time, money, effort, and cognitive load. A Trigger is the cue that prompts the behaviour at the right instant — a notification, a button, or a reminder. If any one of these three is missing or insufficient, the behaviour will not happen, no matter how strong the other two are. A highly motivated person who finds the task too difficult will not act. An easy task with no trigger goes unnoticed. The model also shows that motivation and ability can compensate for each other: very high motivation can overcome moderate difficulty, and extreme simplicity can work even with low motivation.

For marketers and product designers, the model provides a diagnostic tool. When users are not converting, signing up, or engaging, the fix depends on which element is weak. If motivation is high but people still do not act, the problem is usually ability — the process is too complicated or the trigger is missing. This explains why one-click purchasing, pre-filled forms, and push notifications are so effective: they boost ability and provide triggers to people who already have motivation. The framework has shaped the design philosophy of major technology platforms and remains one of the most widely applied behavioural models in UX and digital marketing.
