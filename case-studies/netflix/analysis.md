# Netflix

## What They Do

Netflix is a streaming service with thousands of TV shows and films. They make money through monthly subscriptions. Their entire platform is designed to keep you watching — and keep you subscribed.

## Behavioural Economics Tactics They Use

### Default Nudges (Autoplay)

- **What it is:** A "default" is what happens if you do nothing. Most people stick with the default because changing it takes effort.
- **How Netflix uses it:** When an episode ends, the next one starts automatically in 5 seconds. You'd have to actively press "stop" to not watch it. The default is: keep watching. This is why people binge entire series in one sitting.
- **Study connection:** Study 23 — Nudge (defaults and choice architecture) and Study 30 — Do Defaults Save Lives

### Choice Architecture

- **What it is:** The way choices are arranged affects what you pick. Whoever designs the layout has huge power over your decisions.
- **How Netflix uses it:** Your Netflix homepage is personalised. The shows Netflix most wants you to watch appear at the top, in the biggest thumbnails. The order isn't random — it's carefully designed to guide you towards content that will keep you subscribed.
- **Study connection:** Study 23 — Nudge (choice architecture)

### Mere Exposure Effect

- **What it is:** The more you see something, the more you tend to like it — even if you weren't interested at first.
- **How Netflix uses it:** Netflix shows you the same thumbnail for a show over and over, sometimes changing the image to find the one that grabs your attention. After seeing a show appear on your homepage 10+ times, you're more likely to click on it.
- **Study connection:** Study 33 — Mere Exposure Effect

### Status Quo Bias

- **What it is:** People prefer things to stay the same. Changing feels risky and requires effort, so we stick with what we already have.
- **How Netflix uses it:** Your subscription renews automatically each month. Cancelling means logging in, finding the settings, and actively choosing to stop. Most people just... don't. Even when they're barely watching, the status quo (staying subscribed) wins.
- **Study connection:** Study 32 — Status Quo Bias and Study 10 — Anomalies

### Managing the Paradox of Choice

- **What it is:** When you have too many options, you feel overwhelmed and struggle to choose. You might end up choosing nothing at all.
- **How Netflix uses it:** Netflix has thousands of titles, but they don't show you all of them at once. They curate your homepage into small, themed rows — "Because you watched...", "Trending Now", "Top 10 in the UK". This breaks a huge library into manageable chunks.
- **Study connection:** Study 18 — Paradox of Choice

## Why It Works

Netflix designs every part of the experience so that the easiest thing to do is keep watching and keep paying. Autoplay removes the decision to stop. Personalisation removes the effort of choosing. And status quo bias means you'll stay subscribed unless you actively fight it.
