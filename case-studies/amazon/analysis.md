# Amazon

## What They Do

Amazon is the world's biggest online retailer. They sell almost everything — from books to electronics to groceries. Their website is built to make buying as fast and effortless as possible.

## Behavioural Economics Tactics They Use

### Social Proof

- **What it is:** We trust what other people do. If thousands of people bought something and rated it highly, it must be worth buying.
- **How Amazon uses it:** Every product shows reviews and ratings — "4,523 people bought this" or "4.7 stars from 12,000 reviews." Bestseller badges and "Amazon's Choice" labels add extra reassurance. You're not just buying a product — you're doing what thousands of others did.
- **Study connection:** Study 13 — Influence (Cialdini's social proof principle)

### Anchoring

- **What it is:** The first number you see shapes how you judge everything after it. A high starting price makes the actual price feel like a steal.
- **How Amazon uses it:** Crossed-out original prices are everywhere — "Was £49.99, now £29.99." The £49.99 is the anchor. Even if the product was never really sold at that price for long, your brain now thinks you're saving £20.
- **Study connection:** Study 29 — Heuristics and Biases (anchoring) and Study 20 — Predictably Irrational (anchoring)

### Scarcity and Loss Aversion

- **What it is:** Scarcity means "there isn't much left." Loss aversion means losing something feels about twice as painful as gaining it feels good. Together, they create panic.
- **How Amazon uses it:** "Only 3 left in stock" and "Deal ends in 2 hours" trigger your fear of missing out. You're not just gaining a product — you're avoiding the pain of losing a deal. The countdown timer makes it feel urgent.
- **Study connection:** Study 13 — Influence (scarcity), Study 08 — Prospect Theory (loss aversion)

### Nudges (Reducing Friction)

- **What it is:** A nudge makes the "desired" behaviour easy and the alternative hard. Removing steps between wanting and buying is a powerful nudge.
- **How Amazon uses it:** One-click buying means you go from "I want this" to "I own this" in a single tap. No time to reconsider. "Subscribe & Save" auto-orders products you use regularly, so you never even think about buying them again.
- **Study connection:** Study 23 — Nudge (choice architecture)

### Foot-in-the-Door

- **What it is:** Get someone to say "yes" to something small, and they're more likely to say "yes" to something bigger later.
- **How Amazon uses it:** Amazon Prime starts with a free 30-day trial. That's the small "yes." Once you've been using free next-day delivery for a month, cancelling feels like a loss. The big "yes" — paying £8.99/month — follows naturally.
- **Study connection:** Study 15 — Foot-in-the-Door Technique and Study 08 — Prospect Theory (loss aversion)

## Why It Works

Amazon removes every barrier between wanting something and owning it. Social proof reassures you, anchoring makes the price feel right, scarcity makes you act fast, and one-click buying means there's no pause to think twice. Each tactic alone is powerful — together, they're almost irresistible.
