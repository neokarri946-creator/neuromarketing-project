# Gymshark

## What They Do

Gymshark is a UK-based fitness clothing brand founded in 2012. They sell almost entirely online and built their brand through social media and influencer marketing. They're valued at over £1 billion.

## Behavioural Economics Tactics They Use

### Scarcity Drops (limited stock creates urgency)

- **What it is:** When something is scarce or limited, we want it more. Urgency shuts down rational thinking and pushes us to act fast.
- **How Gymshark uses it:** They release new collections in "drops" with limited quantities. Their website often crashes on launch day because so many people rush to buy at once. Items sell out in minutes, and once they're gone, they're gone. This creates a fear of missing out.
- **Study connection:** Study 13 — Influence (Cialdini's scarcity principle)

### Social Proof (influencer marketing)

- **What it is:** We copy what others do, especially people we admire. If someone we look up to uses a product, we assume it must be good.
- **How Gymshark uses it:** They sponsor fitness influencers with millions of followers — people like David Laid and Whitney Simmons. When these athletes wear Gymshark in every gym video, their audience sees it as the default choice. You don't just see the clothes — you see a lifestyle.
- **Study connection:** Study 13 — Influence (Cialdini's social proof and authority)

### Brand Identity / Self-Congruity (buying who you want to be)

- **What it is:** We buy brands that match the person we want to be — or want others to see us as.
- **How Gymshark uses it:** Wearing Gymshark signals "I'm serious about fitness." Even if you've only been to the gym twice, the brand makes you feel part of the fitness community. The logo becomes an identity badge, not just a label.
- **Study connection:** Study 25 — Brand Identity (we buy what we wanna be)

### Mere Exposure Effect (constant social media presence)

- **What it is:** The more you see something, the more you like and trust it — even if you're not paying attention.
- **How Gymshark uses it:** They post constantly across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. Their influencers post daily. You see the Gymshark logo in your feed over and over. By the time you need gym clothes, Gymshark is the first brand that comes to mind — not because you researched it, but because you've seen it hundreds of times.
- **Study connection:** Study 33 — Mere Exposure Effect

### Commitment and Consistency (the full set effect)

- **What it is:** Once we make a small commitment, we feel pressure to stay consistent with it. Buying one thing makes us more likely to buy more.
- **How Gymshark uses it:** Once you own one Gymshark top, you want the matching shorts. Then the hoodie. Then the bag. Each purchase reinforces your identity as "a Gymshark person" — and consistency says you should keep going.
- **Study connection:** Study 13 — Influence (Cialdini's commitment and consistency)

## Why It Works

Gymshark doesn't just sell clothes — they sell an identity. Social proof tells you it's what fit people wear, scarcity makes you act fast, and once you own one piece, consistency pulls you deeper in. The brand becomes part of who you are, which makes it very hard to leave.
