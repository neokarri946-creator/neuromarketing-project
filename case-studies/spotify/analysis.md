# Spotify

## What They Do

Spotify is a music streaming platform with over 600 million users. They offer a free tier with ads and a premium tier (£10.99/month) without ads. Their algorithm-driven playlists are a huge part of what keeps people hooked.

## Behavioural Economics Tactics They Use

### Foot-in-the-Door (free tier gets you hooked)

- **What it is:** Get someone to say "yes" to something small first. Once they've committed, they're much more likely to say "yes" to something bigger later.
- **How Spotify uses it:** The free tier is the small "yes." You sign up, build playlists, follow artists, and get used to having all your music in one place. Then Spotify offers Premium with a free 1-month trial. By then, you're already invested — saying "yes" feels natural.
- **Study connection:** Study 15 — Foot-in-the-Door Technique

### Mere Exposure Effect (Discover Weekly)

- **What it is:** The more you hear or see something, the more you tend to like it — even if you didn't at first.
- **How Spotify uses it:** Discover Weekly drops 30 new songs into your library every Monday. Songs you skip this week might appear in a different playlist later. The repeated exposure gradually makes unfamiliar music feel familiar and enjoyable.
- **Study connection:** Study 33 — Mere Exposure Effect

### Dopamine Loops (shuffle and variable rewards)

- **What it is:** Unpredictable rewards trigger dopamine — the brain's "wanting" chemical. Not knowing what's next keeps you engaged.
- **How Spotify uses it:** Hitting shuffle is like pulling a slot machine lever. You don't know which song comes next. Sometimes it's perfect, sometimes it's not — that unpredictability keeps you pressing play. Discover Weekly and Release Radar work the same way.
- **Study connection:** Study 07 — Dopamine Economy

### Endowment Effect (your playlists feel like yours)

- **What it is:** Once you own something (or feel like you do), you value it far more than you would otherwise.
- **How Spotify uses it:** After months of building playlists, saving albums, and curating your library, it *feels* like yours. Switching to Apple Music or YouTube Music means losing all of that. The thought of losing your library keeps you locked in.
- **Study connection:** Study 10 — Anomalies (endowment effect)

### Social Proof (what your friends listen to)

- **What it is:** We look at what other people are doing to decide what we should do. If others like it, it must be good.
- **How Spotify uses it:** "Friend Activity" shows what people you know are listening to right now. Spotify Wrapped gets shared millions of times on social media. Playlist follower counts signal popularity. All of this says: *everyone's on Spotify.*
- **Study connection:** Study 13 — Influence (Cialdini's social proof)

## Why It Works

Spotify hooks you with a free tier, builds emotional ownership through your playlists, and keeps you engaged with unpredictable rewards. By the time you consider leaving, you've invested too much to walk away. That's the trap — and it's designed that way.
