# AQA Economics A-Level Spec Map — Overview

## What is this?

This folder maps out exactly where **behavioural economics** appears in the AQA Economics A-Level specification.

It's your cheat sheet for knowing what the exam board wants you to know — and which of your 40 studies and 10 case studies to use as evidence.

## Where does behavioural economics appear in the spec?

Behavioural economics shows up in **two main places**:

- **Paper 1 (Markets and Market Failure)** — Section 3.1.4.8: "Behavioural economics"
- **Paper 3 (Economic Principles and Issues)** — Synoptic essays where you can bring in BE to analyse any topic

It's not a separate module you study on its own. It weaves through everything — market failure, government intervention, consumer behaviour, and policy evaluation.

## Why does it matter for the exam?

- It's **guaranteed content** — the spec lists it explicitly, so it can (and does) come up
- It gives you **evaluation points** that most students miss — saying "this assumes rational behaviour, but behavioural economics shows..." is a strong analytical move
- It lets you **stand out** — linking real studies and evidence to economic theory is exactly what gets top marks

## How to use this spec map

Each file in this folder covers one spec topic. Inside each one you'll find:

- What the spec actually says, explained in plain English
- The key ideas you need to understand
- Which studies (from your 40) to read for evidence
- Which case studies (from your 10) to look at for real-world examples
- An exam tip for using it in answers

**Start with whatever topic you're revising.** You don't have to read them in order. Use them alongside the studies and case studies — they're designed to work together.

You've got this.
